Identify service availability and continuity requirements - Flow ()
A scheduled review within Service Availability & Continuity Management (SACM) triggers the process to identify service availability and continuity requirements, initiated by the service management team. As a result, a comprehensive report detailing the necessary availability and continuity needs for various services is produced and delivered to the relevant stakeholders. This process encompasses the assessment of current service levels and the identification of requirements to ensure that service targets are met consistently. It follows a lifecycle that includes gathering input from various stakeholders, analyzing service performance data, and making informed decisions about future service availability and continuity needs. The process is integral to the Service Availability & Continuity Management capability, ensuring that services remain resilient and reliable. The existence of this process is crucial for maintaining operational stability and meeting customer expectations; without it, the organization risks service disruptions and potential loss of trust from clients. Ultimately, the primary output of this process is the report on service availability and continuity requirements, which is delivered to consuming parties to guide their operational planning.
