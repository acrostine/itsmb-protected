Perform access control - Flow ()
A request for access to sensitive data is initiated by a project manager within Information Security Management (ISM), triggering the Perform access control process. The result of this process is a set of access permissions that are granted or denied based on established security protocols. The Perform access control process ensures that only authorized individuals can access specific information, thereby maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data related to managing and delivering services. This process involves evaluating access requests, making decisions based on security policies, and engaging relevant stakeholders to ensure compliance with organizational standards. It operates within the broader Information Security Management capability, which aims to safeguard critical information assets. This process exists to mitigate risks associated with unauthorized access, thereby protecting the organization from potential data breaches and ensuring regulatory compliance. Without effective access control, the organization would face increased vulnerability to security threats and potential loss of sensitive information. The primary output, access permissions, is delivered to the consuming parties who require access to the information. PROCESS STEPS 1.
