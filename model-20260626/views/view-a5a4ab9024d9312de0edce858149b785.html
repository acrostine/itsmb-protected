Manage major incidents - Flow ()
A major incident is triggered when a critical system outage is reported by a user, prompting the Incident & Service Request Management (ISRM) team to initiate the Manage major incidents process, resulting in a detailed incident report and resolution plan. This report is then communicated to all relevant stakeholders to ensure transparency and coordination during the resolution efforts. The Manage major incidents process encompasses the identification, assessment, and resolution of significant disruptions to service operations, ensuring that the organization can restore services in a timely manner. It involves making critical decisions regarding resource allocation and communication strategies while engaging various stakeholders, including IT support teams and business units, to facilitate effective incident management. This process is integral to the Incident & Service Request Management capability, which aims to maintain service continuity and user satisfaction. This process exists to minimize the impact of major incidents on business operations, thereby safeguarding the organization’s reputation and operational efficiency. Without it, the organization would face prolonged downtimes, leading to customer dissatisfaction and potential financial losses.
