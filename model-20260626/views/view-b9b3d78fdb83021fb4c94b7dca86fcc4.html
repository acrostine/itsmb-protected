Identify service capacity and performance requirements - Flow ()
A request for additional service capacity is initiated by the Capacity Management team in response to anticipated increased demand, resulting in a detailed report outlining the required service capacity and performance requirements. This report is then delivered to the relevant stakeholders for further action. The process of Identify service capacity and performance requirements operates within the Capacity Management (CAPM) capability, focusing on assessing and defining the necessary service levels and performance metrics to meet organizational targets. It involves analyzing current capacity, forecasting future needs, and making informed decisions to ensure alignment with service objectives. Stakeholders include service managers, operations teams, and business units that rely on the defined capacity and performance metrics. This process exists to ensure that the organization can effectively meet service demands without degradation of performance, thereby maintaining customer satisfaction and operational efficiency. Without it, the organization risks overloading systems, leading to service failures or unmet targets. The primary output, the report detailing service capacity and performance requirements, is delivered to consuming parties for implementation. PROCESS STEPS 1.
