Evaluate service availability and continuity - Flow ()
A scheduled review within Service Availability & Continuity Management (SACM) triggers the process to Evaluate service availability and continuity, where the service manager assesses current service performance against established targets, resulting in a comprehensive report on service health and continuity measures. This process systematically evaluates the effectiveness of service availability and continuity strategies, ensuring that the organization can meet its service targets. It involves analyzing service performance data, making decisions on necessary adjustments, and engaging stakeholders to align on service delivery expectations, all within the broader context of the Service Availability & Continuity Management capability. The existence of this process is crucial for maintaining operational resilience and ensuring that service disruptions are minimized. Without it, the organization risks failing to meet service commitments, leading to customer dissatisfaction and potential financial losses. The primary output is the service availability and continuity report, which is delivered to consuming parties for informed decision-making. PROCESS STEPS 1.
