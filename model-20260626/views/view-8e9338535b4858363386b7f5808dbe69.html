Maintain SLAs - Flow ()
A request for a new service level agreement (SLA) is initiated by a service manager within Service Level Management (SLM), leading to the process of Maintain SLAs, which results in updated SLA documentation delivered to the relevant stakeholders. This ensures that all parties have a clear understanding of the expected service quality and performance metrics. The Maintain SLAs process is responsible for defining, evaluating, and updating service level agreements between the organization and its customers or suppliers, ensuring that service catalogues are accurately maintained. It operates within the Service Level Management (SLM) capability, making critical decisions regarding service quality standards and performance expectations, while engaging various stakeholders such as service managers, customers, and suppliers throughout its lifecycle. This process exists to ensure that service quality is consistently monitored and agreed upon, delivering value by aligning expectations between the organization and its stakeholders. Without the Maintain SLAs process, the organization risks miscommunication and dissatisfaction regarding service delivery, ultimately impacting customer relationships and operational efficiency.
