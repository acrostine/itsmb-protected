A request from a customer for an updated service level agreement triggers the Maintain supporting agreements (OLAs and UAs) process, initiated by the Service Level Management (SLM) team, resulting in revised agreements that outline the expected service quality. This ensures that both the service provider and the customer have a clear understanding of their commitments and expectations. The Maintain supporting agreements (OLAs and UAs) process is responsible for defining, evaluating, and updating agreements related to service quality between the organization and its customers or suppliers. It operates within the Service Level Management (SLM) capability, ensuring that service catalogues are accurately maintained and that all agreements reflect current service standards and expectations. This process involves various stakeholders, including service managers, customers, and suppliers, who contribute to the agreement lifecycle. This process exists to ensure that service quality is consistently monitored and agreed upon, delivering value by fostering clear communication and accountability between parties. Without it, the organization risks misunderstandings regarding service expectations, leading to potential dissatisfaction and service delivery issues. The primary output of this process is the updated agreements, which are delivered to the consuming parties involved. PROCESS STEPS 1.
