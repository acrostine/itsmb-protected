Maintain and implement capacity plans - Flow ()
A request for additional server capacity triggered by an anticipated increase in customer transactions initiates the Maintain and implement capacity plans process, which is carried out by the Capacity Management team. As a result, a comprehensive capacity plan is produced that outlines the necessary resources and timelines to meet the expected demand. This process ensures that the organization can effectively manage its resources to maintain sufficient capacity and service performance in alignment with service targets. It involves assessing current capacity, forecasting future needs, and making informed decisions about resource allocation while engaging various stakeholders such as IT operations and service delivery teams. The process is integral to the Capacity Management (CAPM) capability, which supports overall service reliability and performance. The existence of this process is crucial for the organization as it delivers value by preventing service disruptions and ensuring that resources are optimally utilized. Without it, the organization risks over-provisioning or under-provisioning resources, leading to potential service failures or increased costs. The primary output, the capacity plan, is delivered to the consuming parties responsible for implementing the necessary changes. PROCESS STEPS 1.
