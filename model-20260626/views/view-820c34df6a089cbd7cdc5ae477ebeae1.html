Manage incidents and service requests - Flow ()
When a user reports a system outage, the IT support team initiates the Manage incidents and service requests process to address the issue promptly, resulting in a resolution report that details the actions taken and the system's restored functionality. This process is triggered by the incident report and involves coordination among various support staff to ensure a swift response. In general terms, the Manage incidents and service requests process encompasses the identification, logging, and resolution of incidents while also handling user service requests. It follows a structured lifecycle that ensures incidents are prioritized and addressed according to their impact on service delivery, involving stakeholders such as IT support teams and end-users. This process operates within the Incident & Service Request Management (ISRM) capability, which aims to maintain service continuity and user satisfaction. The existence of this process is critical for minimizing downtime and ensuring that user service requests are handled efficiently, thereby maintaining operational effectiveness. Without it, the organization would face prolonged service disruptions and decreased user productivity. The primary output, the resolution report, is delivered to the consuming parties, ensuring they are informed of the incident's status and resolution. PROCESS STEPS 1.
