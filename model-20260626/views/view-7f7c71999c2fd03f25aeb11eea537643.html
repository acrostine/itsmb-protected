Maintain the KEDB - Flow ()
A request to update the Known Error Database (KEDB) is initiated by a Problem Management (PM) analyst following the identification of a recurring incident. This process results in updated entries in the KEDB that provide detailed information about known problems and their workarounds. The Maintain the KEDB process is essential for ensuring that accurate and comprehensive information about known errors is readily available to stakeholders involved in incident resolution. It follows a lifecycle of continuous improvement, where information is regularly reviewed and updated based on new findings or changes in the status of known problems. This process supports the Problem Management capability by facilitating effective communication and knowledge sharing among IT teams. This process exists to enhance the efficiency of incident resolution by providing teams with quick access to known issues and their resolutions, thereby reducing downtime and improving service quality. Without the Maintain the KEDB process, the organization would face increased incident resolution times and a higher likelihood of recurring issues, ultimately degrading service delivery. The primary output is the updated KEDB, which is delivered to consuming parties such as IT support teams and service management. PROCESS STEPS 1.
