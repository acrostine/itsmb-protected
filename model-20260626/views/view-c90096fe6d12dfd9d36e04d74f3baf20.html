Identify problems - Flow ()
A typical scenario for the Identify problems process occurs when a scheduled review within Problem Management (PM) reveals recurring incidents affecting service delivery. The process is initiated by the Problem Management team, leading to the identification of underlying issues that are documented as outputs produced by Identify problems, which are then delivered to relevant stakeholders for further action. In general terms, the Identify problems process aims to systematically uncover and investigate the root causes of issues impacting services, thereby reducing their impact or preventing future incidents. This process operates within the Problem Management capability, making critical decisions about prioritization and resource allocation to address identified problems effectively. Stakeholders involved include IT support teams, service managers, and business units that rely on uninterrupted service delivery. The existence of the Identify problems process is crucial for maintaining service quality and minimizing disruptions within the organization. Without this process, unresolved problems could escalate, leading to increased incident rates and diminished customer satisfaction.
