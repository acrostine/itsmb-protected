Identify information security requirements - Flow ()
A request for a new service implementation triggers the Identify information security requirements process, initiated by the Information Security Management (ISM) team, resulting in a comprehensive set of security requirements tailored for the service. This ensures that all necessary security measures are considered and documented before the service goes live. In general terms, the process identifies and articulates the information security requirements essential for preserving the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information related to managing and delivering services. It involves assessing the specific needs of various stakeholders and making informed decisions that align with the broader Information Security Management capability. This process plays a crucial role in the lifecycle of service delivery, ensuring that security is integrated from the outset. The Identify information security requirements process exists to safeguard the organization against potential security breaches and to ensure compliance with regulatory standards. Without this process, the organization risks exposing sensitive information, leading to potential data loss or reputational damage. The primary output of this process is the documented security requirements, which are delivered to the consuming parties involved in the service implementation. PROCESS STEPS 1.
