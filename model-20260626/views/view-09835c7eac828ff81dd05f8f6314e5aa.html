Handle problems - Flow ()
When a recurring incident is reported by a user, the Handle problems process is initiated within Problem Management (PM) to investigate the underlying issue. This process results in a comprehensive report detailing the identified problem and recommended solutions for stakeholders. In general terms, the Handle problems process aims to identify and investigate problems to mitigate their impact and prevent further incidents. It follows a lifecycle that encompasses problem identification, analysis, and resolution, involving various stakeholders who contribute to the investigation and solution implementation. This process is crucial within the Problem Management capability, which seeks to enhance overall service quality and operational efficiency. The Handle problems process exists to deliver value by minimizing disruptions to services and improving user satisfaction. Without it, organizations would face increased incident rates and prolonged service outages, leading to diminished trust and operational effectiveness.
