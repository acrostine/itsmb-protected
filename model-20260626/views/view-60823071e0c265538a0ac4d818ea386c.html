Maintain and implement service availability and continuity plans - Flow ()
When a scheduled review of service performance reveals potential risks to service availability, the Service Availability & Continuity Management (SACM) team initiates the process to maintain and implement service availability and continuity plans. This results in updated plans that address identified vulnerabilities and ensure continued service delivery. The process of maintaining and implementing service availability and continuity plans encompasses the assessment of current service capabilities, the identification of risks, and the formulation of strategies to mitigate those risks. It follows a lifecycle that includes planning, execution, and monitoring, with decisions made collaboratively among stakeholders such as service managers and IT operations teams. This process is crucial within the Service Availability & Continuity Management capability, which aims to ensure that services meet established availability targets. This process exists to safeguard the organization against service disruptions, thereby maintaining customer trust and operational efficiency. Without it, the organization would face increased risks of service outages, leading to potential financial losses and damage to reputation. The primary output, updated service availability and continuity plans, is delivered to consuming parties who rely on these plans for effective service management. PROCESS STEPS 1.
