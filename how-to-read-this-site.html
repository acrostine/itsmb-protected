Quick glossary
Domain / Grouping
A major area of the business (e.g. "Incident & Problem Management"). Same thing, two names.
Capability
Something the organisation can DO - a standing ability, not a one-off task.
Business Process
A concrete sequence of steps that gets a specific job done.
Realization
"This capability is delivered by that process/application" - a structural fact, not something you need to inspect.
Element type
What KIND of thing a box is (Process, Capability, Application, etc) - shown next to its name.

The drill-down recipe

Worked example: "I found a capability I care about on an overview diagram. How do I see the actual process behind it?"

1
Click the capability's box on the diagram.
2
Open its Analysis tab.
3
Look for a linked view ending in "- Context" or "(processes)" - that's the detailed diagram.
4
Click it.
Honest expectation-setting: this is not a polished one-click "drill into this" button. It's a manual step - open Analysis, scan the list, find the right link. It works, and once you've done it once it's fast, but it's not magic. Now you know the trick.

Search & query

The magnifying-glass icon next to the Model Tree searches every element and view by name.

There's also a SQL query capability built into this kind of published site. Being fully honest: it's there, it's real, it works - it's just not pretty. If you're the curious/technical type and want to query the model directly, it's worth finding. Everyone else can safely ignore it.
